Sports regulations

The founders of the FIA ​​Formula One World Championship (Formula 1) have compiled a set of rules called Sports Regulations, which spell out the provisions of the races, the rights of riders, their responsibilities. Strict compliance with the regulations of the stewards of the International Federation of Motorsport

Key Points

FIA Formula One World Championship or Formula -1 is an annual competition for the world championship in open circuit race cars on wheels. Formula -1 includes individual stages with the status of the Grand Prix. Champions are identified using points scored in the Grand Prix, which summarize at the end of the year. The winner has the highest score. Pilots compete for the world championship title, and F1 teams that take first place receive the Constructors Cup. Participants of the race who missed the competition for no good reason pay a fine.

With the light hand of the French, race cars in the 1980s got their current name - fireballs. Flags of Formula 1

Checkered flag - the end of the race.
Red flag - Stop the race.
Black flag - Disqualification of the rider.
Flag of black and white triangles - Warning to the rider.
A black flag with an orange circle in the center - a message to the rider about the danger of the technical condition of his car.
Yellow single flag - an order to reduce speed and prohibit overtaking in connection with danger.
Yellow double flag - an order to reduce speed and prohibit overtaking, as well as readiness to stop.
Flag with yellow - red stripes - deterioration of the adhesion of the wheels to the road surface due to rain or spilled oil.
The green flag is the end of danger. Resumption of movement.
White flag - a slow car rides along the highway.
The blue flag is a sign to the pilot who they want to overtake.

Points system

From 1 to 25 points get pilots and teams in the Grand Prix for the first 10 places. Points are awarded as follows: 25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1. The first three riders rise to the podium. The number of points in the race is added to the total points of the remaining races in the framework of Formula -1, both for pilots and teams. For reserve pilots, points are credited to their personal account. One team has the right to exhibit up to 4 riders in one season. Each pilot to participate in the Grand Prix must receive a FIA super license.
